Ground

Soil appearance and procedural elevation. Grass instances follow the same height field as the mesh. Water (ocean and ponds) has its own tab.

Soil & albedo

Cloudy soil: base tint plus warm / cool layers. Albedo map blends your texture, or 0 for shader-only.

Elevation (noise)

Fractal noise displaces the ground mesh; higher ridge pushes sharper peaks. Frequency is spatial scale (lower → wider hills). More octaves adds smaller detail frequencies.

Water

Open water and excavated ponds use the water table mesh: animated waves, depth tint, sky reflection, sun specular, foam, and sparkles. Dig terrain below the water line to expose it. Saved in environment JSON as waterShader.

Waves & motion

Primary Gerstner swell, cross chop, ripples, and fine surface detail.

Shoreline & depth

Surface normals, land–water boundary, and how depth tints the water column.

Reflection & specular

Fresnel, sun glint, and sky reflection strength (day vs night).

Foam

Shore breakup and animated foam pattern on shallow water.

Sparkles / glints

Sun-facing sparkles on the water surface.

Transparency

How opaque the water sheet is from different view angles and over foam.

Water colors

Tints for shallow, mid, deep, foam, reflection, glint, and sun spec.

Sky settings

Night: star density and nebula weights. Advanced: every uniform driving the sky shader — sun path, day colors, anti-sun sector, sun disk, sunset, clouds, storm. Names match export key skyDetail in environment JSON.

Sky shader (fine control)

Scroll for all parameters. Sliders use safe min/max; exact values are still saved in exports.

Weather settings

Wind drives grass motion and scrolls sky clouds across the dome. Rain, snow, and lightning add storm weight to the sky; higher lightning = more frequent strikes and brighter cloud flashes.

Flowers

Fish generator (shape & motion)

Same idea as Insects → butterfly motion: changing shape rebuilds the mesh. Depth range is a fraction of the local water column (0 = bottom, 1 = surface).

Lake ecosystem (pellets & food chain)

Pellets are tiny instanced specks (pick a visible color — default orange). They regenerate after fish eat them. Fish lose health over time; they steer toward food and can eat underwater ladybugs (respawn on land) and nip lakeside spider webs.

Insects — butterfly motion

The procedural butterfly swarm (Critters tab) uses global time and Weather → wind speed & direction for drift. Sliders here tune oscillation frequencies and amplitudes (same time base as grass and flowers). Count, colors, and seed stay on Critters.

Procedural generator (shape, noise, wings, legs, eyes)

The mesh is generated in CPU (body, wings, legs, eyes), with surface noise on the body. Wing/leg animation runs in the shader with corrected normals. Emissive keeps thin wings visible. Changing shape or counts rebuilds geometry.

3D path author

Each anchor is a pivot: position + radius (tube thickness at that control). The engine builds a smooth Catmull–Rom curve through your anchors (or a straight segment if you only have two) and extrudes a variable-radius tube. Use this to sculpt vines, tendrils, abstract strokes — all inside the app.

Alt+click the ground (Y=0) to drop an anchor when place mode is on and this panel is open. Default thickness for new anchors is below.

Moon & tides

Simplified lunar tides: two highs per day (semi-diurnal sine), modulated by a slower spring/neap envelope. Raises mean sea level and slightly swells wind waves at high tide. Lake fish use the same mean level.

Spatial memory

Hierarchical frame for how space is remembered and reused: a world slice, a regional slice, then local anchors. Use Ties to spell out how those levels map onto each other (IDs, sectors, terrain features, paths).

Simulation lab

Outline each built-in simulation before you wire it up. Linked to world writes the matching sliders (weather, fish, insects, hives) so the run matches your plan. Spawn the world first if you start from water — counts only apply after land and content spawn.

Audio settings

Elevation brush

Look at the ground and hold left mouse — paint uses the screen center. E exits. Lower digs down to 6 ft (≈1.83 m) below the local surface; the water table is 3 ft (≈0.91 m) down — dig past that to see water. Terrain is saved in this browser; Ctrl+Z (⌘Z on Mac) undoes the last stroke. Stuck with an old map? Use the red button below, or add ?wipe=1 to the address bar and reload.
